# Business Plan — UK Go-To-Market: "Trade-in-a-Box" Subscription for Landscapers

**Author:** Adrien Enjalbert · **Status:** Strategy + GTM blueprint (UK-first) · **Companion to:** `00-MASTER-STRATEGY.md` (LocalLens), `01-BUSINESS-PLAN-ITALY-GTM.md`

> **How this fits LocalLens.** This is the **UK-first market entry** for the LocalLens engine, anchored on a real live client — **Thorburn Landscape** (`thorburnlandscape.co.uk`) — as design partner and first paying customer. It packages presence + leads + tools into one **low-cost subscription** (explicitly **not** rank-and-rent: customers own their brand/site/leads). The managed-ads tier is the near-term **cash engine** while the organic/affiliate rails described in Master Strategy §1–§3 build underneath.

---

## 1. Executive summary

- **Offer:** A **"Trade-in-a-Box"** monthly subscription for UK landscapers/gardeners bundling **everything**: website + Google Business Profile presence + exclusive leads (managed ads) + tools (CRM, reviews, booking, dashboard).
- **Not rent — subscription.** Customers own their site, brand, GBP, and leads. The pitch directly attacks the UK trades' #1 grievance: shared-lead platforms.
- **Wedge proof:** Thorburn Landscape — already built website + managed Google/local ads cheaply. Productise that into a repeatable template; replicate one tradesperson per town.
- **Core strategic tension (and the resolution):** the bundle mixes a **scalable software layer** (≈zero marginal cost) with a **service layer** (managed ads = labour + ad spend per client). **Resolve with tiers** that separate the two, and **always treat ad spend as transparent pass-through.**
- **Pricing (indicative):** Presence **£49–£99/mo** (self-serve SaaS) · Presence + Leads **£199–£349/mo + ad spend** (the core) · Growth **£499+/mo + ad spend**.
- **Why now:** UK trades are actively told (by their own niche press) that *own website + GBP + ads* beats Checkatrade/MyBuilder/Bark on exclusivity, conversion (35–50% vs 15–25%), and cost-per-booked-job. You already deliver exactly that.

## 2. The market (evidence-backed)

### 2.1 The pain: shared-lead platforms
- **Checkatrade:** subscription ~£80–£170/mo (£950–£2,000/yr) just to be listed; no per-lead charge but you compete on the same profile. *(squote, GES 2026.)*
- **MyBuilder / Bark / Rated People:** pay-per-lead credits **£5–£45 per lead**, **shared with 3–5 competitors**, converting at only **15–25%**. *(seoforthetrade, digitaltradies 2026.)*

### 2.2 The agreed-best alternative = what you already do
- UK trade press consensus: **own Google Business Profile + optimised website + ads** delivers **exclusive, higher-intent** leads converting at **35–50%**, at roughly **1/5th the cost per booked job** (~£15–£40 vs £80–£200 on paid platforms). Leads compound for years vs stopping when a subscription ends. *(seoforthetrade 2026.)*
- **This is your unfair advantage:** you already run this stack for Thorburn cheaply. The business is *productising and reselling it as a subscription.*

### 2.3 Pricing anchors (what customers already pay)
- Jobber (software only): ~£23–£199/mo. Tradify: UK/GBP, tax-compliant. Housecall Pro: $79–$349. Checkatrade: ~£120/mo for *listing only*.
- **Implication:** bundling presence + leads + tools lets you anchor high on value while undercutting the *fragmented* total they pay today (Checkatrade + a CRM + a website + ad management).

## 3. The offer & model

### 3.1 The decisive design rule
The bundle contains two cost structures that must be priced differently:
1. **Software layer** (website, CRM, reviews, booking, dashboard) → build once, near-zero marginal cost → **scales infinitely** → drives the cheap entry tier.
2. **Service layer** (managed Google/local ads, GBP optimisation) → labour + ad spend **per client** → **scales with your time** → must be priced for time + de-risked on ad spend.

**Non-negotiables:**
- **Ad spend is always a transparent pass-through**, never absorbed into the subscription. Your fee = management + software.
- **Lead the funnel with the scalable Presence tier**; upsell into managed tiers.

### 3.2 Three tiers

| Tier | Includes | Cost structure | For | Indicative price |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Presence** (SaaS) | Website + GBP setup/optimisation + reviews automation + light CRM + booking, self-serve dashboard | Scalable, near-zero marginal | DIY trades; funnel entry | **£49–£99/mo** |
| **Presence + Leads** (core) | Above **+ managed Google/local ads** | Subscription + **ad spend pass-through** | Thorburn-type; wants leads done-for-them | **£199–£349/mo + ad spend** |
| **Growth** (premium) | Above + multi-channel, priority, reporting calls | Subscription + ad spend | Established trades scaling | **£499+/mo + ad spend** |

### 3.3 Positioning / message
> "Stop renting shared leads from Checkatrade and Bark that go to four of your rivals. For one low monthly fee, own your website, your Google profile, and your leads — plus the software to run every job. Built for landscapers."

Territory exclusivity (**one landscaper per town**) is a strong differentiator borrowed from rank-and-rent logic, applied within a subscription — it protects lead quality and justifies premium tiers.

## 4. Thorburn as design partner → replication template

1. **Productise the current setup** into a repeatable "Landscaper Setup Kit":
   - Website template (landscaping/garden-maintenance)
   - GBP optimisation checklist
   - Google/local ad campaign templates (e.g. `garden maintenance [town]`, `landscaping near me`, `fencing/decking [town]`)
   - Review-request automation flows
   - Light CRM + booking defaults
2. **Build the case study:** instrument call tracking + dashboard → "N exclusive leads/mo, £X cost per booked job vs Checkatrade." This is the single most persuasive sales asset for the next gardener.
3. **Replicate by town, one tradesperson per area** → exclusive territory; no lead cannibalisation.
4. **Feed LocalLens:** this vertical is the first live instance of the flywheel (presence → traffic → leads → reviews → ranking). Managed ads = near-term cash; organic + affiliate rails (Master Strategy §1) layer in as traffic grows.

## 5. Unit economics (illustrative — validate with real numbers)

- **Presence tier:** ~all-margin after build; hosting/tooling ~£5–£15/client/mo. Pure scalable SaaS.
- **Managed tier:** subscription £199–£349 is for *your time + software*; ad spend (£300–£1,000+/mo typical for a local trade) flows through at cost. Target: keep management time per managed client low via templates so gross margin stays healthy.
- **Customer value math (the pitch):** if exclusive leads convert at ~40% and a landscaping job is worth £hundreds–£thousands, even a handful of booked jobs/month dwarfs the fee — the same ROI logic UK trade press uses against Checkatrade.
- **Targets to instrument from client #1:** leads/mo, cost per booked job, % calls answered, churn, management hours/client, tier mix.

## 6. Sequencing (UK-first)

- **Phase 0 — Fix & instrument (now):** **thorburnlandscape.co.uk is returning HTTP 502 — confirm/fix the host immediately** (a down site kills ad Quality Score + leads today). Add call tracking + a simple leads/spend dashboard.

- **Phase 4 — Engine + expand (mo 6+):** wire LocalLens loops (reviews → ranking → organic traffic → affiliate/lead rails); expand to adjacent trades (fencing, driveways, tree surgery) and/or the Italy plan.

## 7. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Why it matters | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Service layer caps scale | Managed ads = your time/client | Push self-serve Presence; templatise ad setup; add VA/hire only against managed revenue |
| Ad-spend confusion/blame | Clients conflate fee with budget | Transparent pass-through + dashboard (leads vs spend) |
| Lead-quality disputes | Trades blame system if *they* miss calls | Call tracking + answered/missed visibility; set expectations up front |
| **Thorburn site down (502 now)** | Hurts live ads + leads + your proof case | **Fix host immediately** before scaling |
| No-code ceiling for multi-tenant | Many client sites/dashboards need real infra | OK for first ~10 clients; multi-tenant build with co-founder by Phase 3 |
| Google dependency | Algo/ads shifts hit all clients at once | Own CRM/reviews layer (not ads-only); diversify channels over time |
| Churn if value unclear | Trades cancel what they don't understand | Monthly leads/ROI report; month-to-month builds trust vs Checkatrade contracts |
